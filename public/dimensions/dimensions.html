DIMENSIONS

In the 3D world of Minecraft, there are realms called dimensions. These dimensions contain biomes, structures, mobs, and more, all unique to their respective realms. From the lively nature of the Overworld to the fiery hellscape of the Nether and the mysterious, empty expanse of the End, each dimension is unique in its own way, offering players different experiences as they explore the world.

THE NETHER

The Nether is an environment completely different to that of the Overworld. Likened to the underworld, it is a fiery hellscape containing an ocean of lava, with its ground consisting mainly of netherrack. Water evaporates in this biome, and there is not much life; the creatures that do exist are all either hostile or attack when threatened. In the Nether, rest is not an option. You must traverse the hellish landscape in order to gather unique resources, and you must face formidable foes in the process. If you wish to obtain the ancient ores, or slay the fearsome dragon that sleeps in the End, you must venture into the Nether.

THE OVERWORLD

The Overworld is an environment very similar to that of our own Earth. With 54 distinct biomes, its landscape varies a lot from place to place. Teeming with life, the Overworld is home to all passive mobs, as well as many neutral and hostile mobs. It is the dimension that you spawn in when you first load the map, and it contains all that you need to survive and thrive. In Minecraft, the Overworld is your refuge and your home. THE END

Unlike the other two dimensions, the End is a barren, empty space. Surrounded by void, the End is like an ocean of void with islands of endstone scattered about. The sky is perpetually dark, with no sun or moon visible. The only mobs that spawn here are Endermen, and the only life that spawns are the tall trees of chorus plant. The only thing of note in these desolate lands is the abandoned end cities, which contain valuable loot, as well as the fearsome Ender Dragon hidden in the center of the End, which must be defeated to complete the game.
