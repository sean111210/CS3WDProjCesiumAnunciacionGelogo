SURVIVAL MODE

Survival Mode is the default gamemode of Minecraft. In this gamemode, players have to survive, building and crafting items, tools, and blocks. There is a health bar as well as a hunger bar that slowly depletes over time, and an oxygen bar that appears when you are underwater. When you run out of hearts in your health bar, you die and return to your spawn point.

ADVENTURE MODE

This gamemode is similar to Survival Mode. However, your interaction is limited. You can interact with all mobs, but you can only interact with certain objects like levers and doors, and place blocks only on certain other blocks. This gamemode is often used for custom maps, with the restrictions preventing players from cheating or griefing/destroying maps.

SPECTATOR MODE

In Spectator Mode, you can effectively fly freely, clipping through blocks and entities. However, you cannot interact with blocks, entities, or their own inventory, as their hotbar is invisible. You do not have a hunger or health bar in this gamemode.

CREATIVE MODE

Creative Mode is where the "sandbox" part of Minecraft truly comes in. In Creative Mode, you can do whatever you want, with access to an infinite amount of blocks and items, and almost complete invulnerability.You can use items and blocks normally inaccessible to the player in Survival Mode, and can fly around, though you cannot clip through blocks and entities.

HARDCORE MODE

Hardcore is like survival mode, but with a much greater challenge. The difficulty is locked to hard, and players only have one life. When you die in hardcore mode, you cannot respawn; instead, you can only spectate the world you were playing in. This gamemode is for players looking for the ultimate survival challenge.

DIFFICULTIES

PEACEFUL

Peaceful is the least challenging mode in Minecraft and it is created to be played easy. The appearance of hostile mobs is completely absent, your health is automatically replenished, and hunger does not deplete, hence you cannot die of starvation or beatings by the enemy. The mode is best suited to constructing, experimenting with, or introducing oneself to the mechanics of the game without the need to worry, though it hinders progress, since hostile mobs cannot drop items, so some objectives such as reaching the End cannot be achieved without modifying the difficulty.

EASY

Easy presents elements of survival and is yet forgiving. Enemy crowds are created, but they do not cause much harm, and being hungry may decrease your health but cannot kill you. Zombies can hardly smash wooden doors, and negative status effects like poison will not be lethal. The challenge is good among beginners who would want to enjoy combat and survival without serious repercussions of errors.

NORMAL

Normal offers the typical Minecraft survival game and is the default setting of majority of worlds. Video enemies cause moderate damage with hostile mobs, and ignorance is capable of killing you in hunger, zombies can even break through wooden doors. Status effects are even fatal, and crowds can possess more sophisticated behaviors, like zombies calling reinforcements. This is a challenge of medium difficulty that must be trained and focused on yet not too hard to be accomplished by most players.

HARD

Hard is a considerable difficulty and is meant to be played by the experienced players. Mobs are much more damaging, hunger wears out quicker and kills you, and zombies have always the ability to break wooden doors. The probability of meeting armored mobs, baby zombies, and other threatening types of them is larger, and whenever killed, villagers become zombie villagers. Boss fights are also more intense and survival cannot be achieved without a good strategy, planning and good equipment.
