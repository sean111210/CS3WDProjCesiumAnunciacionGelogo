MOBS

Mobs are an integral part of the Minecraft experience. As AI-contolled entities, they are littered all around the game world and contribute to its dynamic environment. From the villager and sheep to the creeper and zombie, mobs add depth and excitement to gameplay. These mobs can drop various items upon death, whether it's resources, food, or rare loot, helping the player in various ways. Mobs can be broadly categorized into three types, each exhibiting distinct behaviors and interactions with players.

MOB TYPES

PASSIVE

Passive or friendly mobs are generally non-aggressive and will not attack the player unless provoked. They are docile and will not attack the player, regardless if they are provoked or attacked. Most of these types of mobs can be bred and used for resources.

NEUTRAL

Neutral mobs are overall passive and will not attack the player unless provoked. They will attack other mobs, but they are not hostile towards the player unless attacked first.

HOSTILE

Hostile mobs are dangerous, aggressive and will attack the player on sight. They are hostile towards the player and will attack them without provocation, each one having unique behaviors and characteristics.
